I can spot a “personal growth” post in 3 seconds. Here’s how:
1. Starts with a deep quote that isn’t deep.
 “Be yourself, everyone else is taken.” Thanks, Pinterest.
2. Follows with “I’ve been reflecting lately…”
No, you’ve been scrolling.
3. Mentions “burnout” and “alignment” in the same paragraph.
4. Uses 3 emojis per sentence.
 🔥✨💪 — the Holy Trinity of fake motivation.
5. Says “I don’t usually share this, but…”
You usually do. That’s your whole content plan.
6. Every lesson magically ends with self-promotion.
“I learned to love myself → and that’s how I built my 7-figure agency.”
7. “It’s not about success. It’s about fulfillment.”
 And yet, your entire feed screams “Please notice my success.”
8. The humblebrag sandwich:
“Grateful, but not satisfied.” 
Translation: “I’m better than you, but spiritual about it.”
9. “If this helped one person, it was worth it.”
It didn’t.
10. And my favorite:
They write like philosophers…
but comment like salespeople.
People don’t post their truth anymore.
They post their strategy.
