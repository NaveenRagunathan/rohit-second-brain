My top-performing LinkedIn posts from the last 30 days
always starts with word 'I' or 'My'
Real stories. Real experiences. The stuff that actually happened.
Do they bring in clients directly? Rarely.
Do they make the right people feel like they know me? Every time.
So I've been experimenting with something: writing my sales posts the same way.
Not "here's my offer." But "here's what I was going through when I built it."
Same opening. 
Same personal pull. 
Just with somewhere to go at the end.
Still testing. But the early numbers are interesting.
The key thing you're doing here is naming a real pattern you noticed, which makes people trust you before you even get to the point. 
That's the whole game with this format.
One thing I'd push you on though: don't chase the impression number too hard on the sales posts. 
The personal posts earn trust, the sales posts spend it. 
You need both, but they're doing different jobs. 
If you blur them too much you can end up with content that performs okay but converts nothing.
Use the "I" opener to tell the story of why the thing exists. Then let the offer be the natural landing place. 
That's the move.
