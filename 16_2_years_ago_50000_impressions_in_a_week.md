2 years ago: 50,000+ impressions in a week.
This week: 13,000.
2 years ago I would've looked at 13,000 and felt really bad.
But this time it feels really good.
Because now, I'm not comparing it to what I used to do. I'm respecting the fact that I started again.
I don't talk about this enough.
But starting is easy when nobody knows what you're capable of. 
Starting again when your ego remembers what you used to do?
That's f*cking humbling.
Your ego remembers the old numbers. 
But LinkedIn doesn't care what you used to do.
Maybe this isn't about LinkedIn.
Maybe it's about the gym you stopped going to. 
The business you paused. 
The content you abandoned. 
The skill you got rusty at.
The version of you you're trying to rebuild.
Starting over doesn't mean you failed. 
It means you still give a shit.
What have you been too proud to start again?
