If I don't post for 7 days, my business runs fine.
I'm not relying on social media.
I stopped treating content like it was the whole game.
85% of what actually runs a business is stuff nobody ever sees. 
The relationships, the partnerships, the systems, the delivery. 
Content is the 15% people see, so they assume it's the whole thing. 
It's not even close.
The real game is distribution. 
Not followers. Not views. 
Who can put you in front of the right people.
Say your client is an executive. 
Instead of writing LinkedIn posts hoping one lands on their feed, ask yourself who already has access to them. 
An executive coach. 
A service provider already selling to that room. 
A company employee two handshakes away. 
A local event they actually show up to.
Real things. Not post and pray.
Last year I started reaching out to Skool community owners, coaches running their own communities, and partnered with them as their design agency. 
Anyone who subscribed got their basic LinkedIn brand built by my team. 
They booked a slot, filled out a form, and over time we became the obvious choice because they trusted the coach who introduced us.
Full circle. Still getting clients from it now.
Jasmin Alić says it best. It's not B2B. It's P2P. 
People to people.
Most people skip all of this and go straight to content, hoping the algorithm does the distribution work for them. 
It won't. Not consistently.
Build the distribution network side by side as you are grinding on LinkedIn.
