In May, 9 months later → I hit $27,799 in revenue. 
That was my best month ever. 
And yeah, it felt good. 
But here’s the part most people don’t talk about: 
A month before that, I made $19K. 
Before that? Just $8K. 
And right after that peak? It dropped to $17K again. 
That’s the reality of running your own business. 
It’s not always up and to the right. 
Some months you feel unstoppable. 
Other months you wonder if you’re doing something wrong. 
The internet doesn’t show you this. 
It shows the highlight reel. 
What most people didn't share: 
- That big month took me 9 months to reach 
- It’s revenue, not profit (I have expenses, team, taxes) 
- And some days, I still feel like I’m figuring it all out 
I’m sharing this for anyone who’s in a low month right now. 
- Maybe you’re making $3K and wondering if it’s enough. 
- Maybe you’re working nonstop and barely covering costs. 
- Maybe you’re quietly comparing yourself to people online and feeling behind. 
Please don’t. You’re not behind. 
You’re in your build-up phase. 
You’re learning how to ride the waves. 
Feast and famine is part of the journey. 
What matters is that you keep going → even when it’s quiet. 
P.S. I still haven’t hit a $30K month. 
But I’ve come a long way from hoping to hit $3K. 
And I’m proud of that.
