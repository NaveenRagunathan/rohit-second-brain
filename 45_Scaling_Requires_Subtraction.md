Scaling Requires Subtraction.
Growth isn’t about doing more.
At first, I thought scaling meant adding more.
More people, more offers, more clients…
Until I realized—growth was slowing.
The more I added, the heavier it got.
So I did the opposite.
I started subtracting.
🔻 I killed unnecessary processes.
Meetings that led nowhere? Gone.
Layers of approval? Slashed.
🔻 I cut my team in half.
Too many hands, but not enough impact.
A-players don’t need babysitting.
🔻 I dropped half my service offerings.
More services = diluted expertise.
I doubled down on what I do best.
🔻 I fired bad clients.
If they drained my energy, they had to go.
Less stress, more profits.
The result? More clarity. More speed. More growth.
Scaling isn’t about doing more.
It’s about doing the right things, better.
What do you need to subtract? 👇
