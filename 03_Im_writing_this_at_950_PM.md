I'm writing this at 9:50 PM.
If you're in a dark place right now, keep reading.
A few years ago, I was consistently making $6K months as a designer.
I'm from India. A $6K month is huge.
But I got impatient.
I started seeing posts online.
People making $100K a month.
And the greed in me thought, there's a cheat code here.
These people have some secret pill. 
That's how they market it, right?
So I started buying courses.
One after another.
I invested most of what I'd saved.
Nothing worked.
While I was spending, I was losing clients too.
I started thinking I had just gotten lucky. 
That I was never actually good enough.
One day, I was sitting with my friend. I told him, "Man, I think I'm losing. I've tried everything. I don't know how this works anymore."
In male friendships, there's usually zero emotion. 
We just pull each other's legs and move on.
But he looked at me and said:
"Rohit, you're losing? A year ago, you told me if you could pay for Wi-Fi and food, you'd do this forever. What happened to that guy?"
That hit different.
I thought, fuck it.
I'm the guy who did it once.
I can do it again.
The next day, nothing had changed.
Same situation.
But I went in full force anyway.
Same things, but this time I just knew I would figure it out.
That belief has energy in it.
I told myself, I'll bang my head against the wall if I have to.
But I'm getting this done.
Six months later, I hit my first $10K month.
This wasn't the first time I lost hope in myself.
It's happened more times than I can count.
But every time, I come back to the same thing.
Sometimes, I only have me.
And I have to be the one to pull myself up.
Because if I lose hope, the people following in my footsteps lose hope too.
I can't let that happen.
So I'll bang my head against the wall if I have to.
I'll fucking do it.
