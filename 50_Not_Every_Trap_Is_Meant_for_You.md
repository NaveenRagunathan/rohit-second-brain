Not Every Trap Is Meant for You… 
But You Better Be Prepared.
Life has a way of testing you when you least expect it.
One night, I was riding home from a party.
It was 11 PM, and the streets were unsettlingly quiet.
Then I noticed something strange.
Groups of three men, stationed at different points along the road,
All wearing black masks.
Not just three men - 25 of them, 
scattered strategically, watching the street.
My gut told me: This isn’t good.
As I got closer to my house, 
my heart started pounding.
Another group was near my doorstep.
And they weren’t empty-handed.
Each of them carried a knife, wrapped in paper.
In India, it’s rarely about guns.
It’s about close, raw combat.
When you see something like this,
you don’t wait to find out what happens next.
I rushed into my house, locked the doors,
and called my friends to figure out what was happening.
Turns out, they weren’t after us.
They were waiting for someone else,
tied to an incident earlier in the day.
But here’s the thing:
Even though I knew it wasn’t about me,
I also knew I had friends who might get me involved.
And in moments like these, 
you realize just how fragile stability can be.
That night, as the adrenaline wore off,
I felt something deeper than fear gratitude.
When life is peaceful, don’t take it for granted.
Now, when my team asks me,
“Why are you always so happy?”
That’s why.
I’ve been through worse.
I’ve faced chaos head-on.
And I’ve learned not to let dumb stuff steal my peace.
Life’s too short for that.
If you can sip your coffee in peace, without worry
remember, many don’t have that luxury. 
Appreciate it.
