I've worked with 80+ clients from LinkedIn.
None of them daily like my post.
That shouldn't matter to you, and here's why.
LinkedIn is mostly filled with people trying to grow their own brand. 
So the likes and comments you get are mostly peers. 
Other creators. People in the same boat as you.
Your actual buyer is lurking. 
Reading. Not engaging.
If you're optimising for likes, you're optimising for the wrong person.
What I do instead:
1. Big fish strategy
Every industry has a gold standard. One person everyone follows. One name that holds the attention of the exact audience you want.
Get on their radar. Write a thoughtful post. Tag them genuinely. Reference their work in a way that adds something. When they engage, their entire audience (full of your ICP) sees your name.
One right relationship outperforms a hundred random likes.
2. Find the small active creators who already have your audience
They don't need a huge following. They need to be active and connected to the right people.
Build real relationships with them. When they engage with your content, it surfaces to their network. And their network is who you're trying to reach.
3. Just connect with more of your ICP directly
Simple but underused. Send connection requests to the people you actually want as clients. Exchange a few genuine DMs. LinkedIn's algorithm starts showing them your content because of that signal.
You don't need viral reach.
You need the right 500 people to keep seeing your name.
Most people on LinkedIn are creating content and hoping. 
This is how you make sure the right people actually see it.
Hope that helps ❤️
