I usually say yes to things that scare me a little.
That's the only reason I took on my first coaching client.
He was a young CFP who reached out wanting to learn how I was getting clients from LinkedIn. 
I remember reading his message and thinking "Why is he asking me?" I was a designer who'd figured out a few things through trial and error. Not a coach.
But I said yes. 
Poker face in front of him.
Inside panicked.
That night I was on YouTube searching things like:
"How do great coaches run sessions?"
"What questions should coaches ask?"
"How do you structure a coaching call?"
I thought that made me unqualified. 
Now I think it meant I cared enough to prepare.
The mistake isn't learning from people further ahead than you. 
The mistake is copying the tactics without understanding the thinking behind them.
That first session taught me three things I still use.
1. Ask more questions than you think you need to.
2. Shut up more than feels comfortable.
3. People don't need you to impress them. They need to feel understood.
Hope it helps!
