5,000 followers. 100+ likes a post. 0 clients that month.
I've lived that.
Slow months. 
0 new clients.
Days of nothing. Just frustration.
Then someone booked a call.
I jumped on immediately. 
Wrong fit. 
Wanted to negotiate the price down before we even started.
But my desperation was screaming.
So I took him anyway at a lower price.
Better to have something than nothing, right?
Wrong..
Three weeks later he's arguing about revisions. Blaming my team for everything. Demanding more time than the contract says.
One desperate decision. Three months of damage.
I've made this mistake more times than I want to admit.
Every time I took the wrong client out of desperation, it cost me more than the empty month.
The lesson isn't about better boundaries or qualifying harder.
It's this: the version of you making decisions from fear shouldn't be running your business.
Hold your standard. Even when it's uncomfortable. Especially then.
The slow months are real. They pass. Your reputation doesn't reset as easily.
Ever said yes out of desperation? How did it play out?
