Burn the boats.
Shut down the safety net. Go all in. Prove you believe in yourself.
It's the most shared advice in the creator economy.
A lot of people told me to shut down the agency before I started coaching.
For a while, I felt ashamed that I didn't.
But I wasn't scared of hard work. 
I wasn't scared of failing publicly. 
I wasn't scared of starting over.
I was scared of desperation.
There's a version of you that shows up when your back is against the wall financially. 
I'd met that version of myself before, 
and I didn't like him.
He stops thinking long term because he needs this month to work. 
And people can feel it. 
That energy makes you tolerate clients you shouldn't, 
Say things you don't fully believe, 
Take rejection personally because your survival is attached to it.
So I kept the agency. (It was fully booked btw)
Now I could turn down the wrong client . - because I have safety net.
Hold my price when someone pushed back. - because I have safety net.
Post content that flopped without reading it as a sign I should quit.- because I have safety net.
If you're building something new 
and you've got something working on the side, 
don't leave it until the new thing actually has legs. 
Yes it's double the work. 
Yes it's tiring. 
But it puts you in a stronger position than 80% of people who burned their boats too early.
Don't be afraid of the hard work.
