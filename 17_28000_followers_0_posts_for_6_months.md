28,000 followers. 0 posts for 6 months.
I started again 7 days ago.
The first thing I remembered: numbers in your hook line change everything.
Not "here's what I've been thinking about."
Not "something shifted for me recently."
Something like "28,000 followers and I ghosted them for 6 months."
Or "24 likes on my comeback post. Still posted the next day."
Or "2 years ago I was posting 3x a day on LinkedIn. Then I stopped completely."
Numbers stop the scroll because your brain can't ignore them. 
They're specific. 
Specific feels real. 
Real feels worth reading.
And they have to be true. That's the whole point.
So next time you're staring at a blank post, lead with a number that's true and slightly uncomfortable to share.
That discomfort is usually the signal you've got something worth saying.
Try it, let me know if you get more engagement than usual.
