$8,600 from one client.
A few years ago? I didn’t even think this was remotely possible.
Back then, I was charging $200 for projects I now do for fun.
And still doubting myself every step of the way.
I didn’t know how to price.
I didn’t feel “ready.”
And if I’m honest, I didn’t believe people would ever pay me that kind of money.
But here’s what changed everything: Proof
As Alex Hormozi says—
“If you don’t have confidence, build proof.”
That became my motto.
I stopped chasing validation…
And started focusing on work I was proud of.
Project after project, no matter the budget
I gave it everything.
$200 client?
They got my best.
$500 client?
Still my best.
Because I believed this one thing:
If you keep doing great work, even when no one’s watching—
Eventually, someone will.
I still follow this rule.
That big project wasn’t luck.
It was the result of years of giving the best service.
If you’re in that early stage now
charging less than you’re worth
Don’t stress about the price yet.
Focus on building undeniable proof.
Give your best to each and every client.
Because when the confidence kicks in?
The clients won’t ask for discounts.
They’ll ask for your invoice.
Just sharing this -
In case it helps the old me who's still out there trying.
