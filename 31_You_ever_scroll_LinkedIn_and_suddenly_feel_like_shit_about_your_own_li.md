You ever scroll LinkedIn and suddenly feel like shit about your own life?
“$10K in 10 days.”
“$100K in 3 months.”
And you think:
damn, I’m working every day.
Why isn’t it happening for me?
I know that feeling.
Because I lived it.
I was making $500 a month.
Couldn’t buy a single thing I wanted.
Wondering if I should quit.
But in my head
I was the best.
I told myself my clients were lucky to have me.
That my work was gold.
That my time was priceless.
Was it true?
Not really.
Huh, but it kept me alive.
Here’s the truth:
If you let their stories
make you feel small,
you’ll start believing it.
You’ll forget who you are.
You’ll lose your voice.
Even if you hit the numbers,
you’ll hate yourself.
So be f*king delusional.
- Not arrogant.
- Not cocky.
Just certain.
Certain you’re the best.
Even when nobody sees it.
Because the alternative
is believing you’re nothing.
And that’s how most people lose.
Don't lose.
