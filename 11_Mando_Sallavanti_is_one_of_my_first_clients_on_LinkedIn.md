Mando Sallavanti is one of my first clients on LinkedIn.
Without that client, none of this exists.
Two years ago I had 3,000 followers and zero niche. 
I was selling content design to anyone who'd have me. 
Great clients, some of the top creators in the space.
Everyone kept telling me to niche down.
But when rent is due, you don't say no to clients.
So I took whatever came through the door.
Then one of my clients referred me to Mando.
Mando liked the work and referred me to two more financial advisors. 
Those two referred me to more. 
And one day I looked at my client list and realised 70% of my clients were financial advisors. (other 30% are agency owners)
I hadn't gone looking for any of them.
I just worked with whoever showed up, 
did good work, and asked for referrals. 
Some clients like Mando referred me without even being asked. 
Those are the ones that change everything.
I didn't choose my niche. At that time, I couldn't even afford to. 
But I did good work for every client who came through, 
and eventually my niche found me.
You can't think your way to a niche when rent is due. 
You work your way to one.
If you are in the same spot then stop waiting for clarity. 
Start with the next client in front of you. 
Do good work. 
Pay attention to where the referrals go.
One good client can change everything.
Mando, if you're reading this, thank you. 
You changed the trajectory.
